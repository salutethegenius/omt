The Ledger Mark

The M is constructed from two vertical columns and two diagonal strokes — exactly as a structured M should be in a serif context, but abstracted to geometric blocks. The green ledger line runs the full width of the mark at the mid-point.

That horizontal rule is the differentiating element. It references the accounting ledger, the balance sheet dividing line, and the ruled paper of financial records — without depicting any of them literally.

  • "Max" — Libre Baskerville 700, upright, navy
  • "Tax" — Libre Baskerville 700, italic, green
  • Italic "Tax" signals action — the service being performed
  • Green ledger line: full-width at mark midpoint
  • Sub: Plus Jakarta Sans 500, all caps, +1.5px tracking
  • Mark stands alone as favicon without wordmark

The Column Mark

Four vertical rectangles of ascending heights with a green cap bar across the top of the tallest — the T crossbar in hidden form. The ascending columns read as a bar chart, as architectural columns, as financial growth over time.

The green cap doesn't just mark the T. It marks the ceiling — the maximum, the peak, the "max" in MaxTax. Every element earns its place.

  • 4 columns: short / tall / medium / accent
  • Green cap: spans 3 tallest — T crossbar geometry
  • Baseline rule: green, 40% opacity — the ledger floor
  • Column 4: 30% opacity — secondary/shadow column
  • Reads as growth chart at a glance, letters on closer look

The Bracket Mark

The accounting bracket [ ] is how CPAs close a balance sheet. The brackets frame the MT letterforms, both protecting and presenting them. This mark says: everything is contained, everything balances.

The green T-crossbar is the only colour deviation. It marks the service — tax — as the active, performed element within the structured bracket system.

  • Outer brackets: 4px vertical + 16px horizontal arms
  • Interior MT: same proportional stroke weight
  • Green T-crossbar only — everything else navy
  • Requires minimum 48px for full legibility
  • Best suited to print — letterhead, proposals, PDF covers
  • Not recommended as primary favicon

Why This Palette Signals Premium Advisory

Deep navy signals financial authority without the coldness of black or the genericness of corporate blue. It is the colour of trust documents, official letterhead, and serious financial institutions. It says: we have been here, we will be here.

Ledger green is not an accent — it is a second primary. It marks everything in motion: the service, the CTA, the growth indicator. Muted enough to feel professional; distinctive enough to differentiate from every navy-and-blue competitor in the Orlando market.

The M-with-green-rule is immediately ownable in the Orlando market. No other accounting firm has this geometry. The ledger line communicates exactly what you do — without showing a calculator, a dollar sign, or a scale.
